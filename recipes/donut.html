Donut Recipe

Description

Donuts are a beloved pastry characterized by their soft, airy texture and distinctive shape. Most commonly, they come in a ring shape with a hole in the center, though they can also be filled with various sweet fillings like jam, cream, or custard. The exterior is typically slightly crispy, while the interior remains tender and fluffy.

Donuts can be either baked or fried, with each method offering a different texture and flavor profile. They are often coated or topped with a variety of glazes, such as classic sugar, chocolate, or maple, and adorned with sprinkles, nuts, or other toppings to enhance their appeal.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Sugar
  • Yeast (for yeast-raised donuts) or Baking Powder/Baking Soda (for cake donuts)
  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Butter or Oil
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Mix flour, sugar, yeast (or baking powder), eggs, milk, and butter to form the dough.
  2. Knead the dough until smooth and let it rise in a warm place until it has doubled in size.
  3. Roll out the dough and cut it into shapes using a cutter or mold.
  4. Fry the doughnuts in hot oil or bake them in a preheated oven until they are golden brown.
  5. Transfer the doughnuts to a wire rack to cool completely.
  6. Coat the cooled doughnuts with glaze, icing, or other desired toppings.
